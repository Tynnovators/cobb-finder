The Idea

What is the problem we are trying to solve?

Scoliosis is a disorder that causes an abnormal curve of the spine. Depending on the severity of the curve and the risk for it getting worse, scoliosis can be treated with observation, bracing, or surgery. Doctors make the decision of whether to perform surgery or not by calculation of Cobb Angle.

The Cobb angle is the most widely used measurement to quantify the magnitude of spinal deformities, like in the case of scoliosis, on plain radiographs. Conventionally, the measurement of Cobb Angle is done manually by drawing of lines along the endplates in the X Ray, finding their intersection, and using an angle tool to measure the angle between the endplates, which is essentially the Cobb Angle.

In this project, we are trying to automate the calculation of the Cobb Angle from a given Ground Truth Image.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is Scoliosis?

    Scoliosis is a disorder that causes an abnormal curve of the spine. It is a sideways curvature of the spine that occurs most often during the growth spurt just before puberty.

  • What is the "Cobb Angle"?

    The Cobb angle is the most widely used measurement to quantify the magnitude of spinal deformities, like in the case of scoliosis, on plain radiographs. Conventionally, the measurement of Cobb Angle is done manually by drawing of lines along the endplates in the X Ray, finding their intersection, and using an angle tool to measure the angle between the endplates, which is essentially the Cobb Angle.

  • What is a "Ground Truth Image"?

    Ground truth is a common terminology that is widely used in various fields to basically refer to any kind of information provided by direct observation.

  • What process is followed to detect the Cobb Angle from the Ground Truth Image?

    CobbFinder uses Computer Vision Technology to calculate Cobb Angles from Ground Truth Image. The ground truth image uploaded by the user is generally colored, so we first convert it to a greyscale image. Then, we perform thresholding, which is a type of image segmentation, to convert the image from a greyscale one to binary image. After this, edge detection is performed using the Canny Edge Filter (since we need edges to find the center line). Once that is done, we perform contour detection to get the contours by averaging the x co ordinates of the points having the same y co ordinates. Lastly, there is a need to perform smoothing, because contours are detected in groups, and the center line that we extract by contour detection is a bit thicker. At this point, we are able to detect the center line. We can then find the Cobb Angles from this center line by calculating perpendicular slopes on the smoothened centerline we just obtained.
